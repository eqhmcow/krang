Edit Story

The Edit Story page is where you enter and edit all story content.

The elements included on the Edit Story page are the body of your Story, such as paragraphs, images, and links to other stories.

On the Edit Story page, there are four parts. They are:

  1. Properties
  2. Content
  3. Sites/Categories
  4. Actions

Properties

The top box on the Edit Story page has the Story's properties and its Contributors. Some of this information is not editable.

  • URL: The URL field is controlled by the CMS. Although it appears as a non-editable field, it can be changed by modifying the fields that make up the URL. These fields are the site and category.
  • Type: Each Story has a Type. This is set by the CMS on creation of a New Story and is not an editable field. If you selected the incorrect Story Type, you will need to return to 'New Story.' Story Type selection determines the elements that are available on the Edit Story page.
  • Story Id: Story ID. Set by the CMS on creation of a New Story. This field cannot be changed.
  • Title: This is the Title of your Story. Depending on the Story Type it may appear directly in the output. The Title is a required field.
  • Slug: The slug is a one-word identifier used as the last part of the Story's URI, and the directory in which the story resides. Some Story Types require a slug. For those that do not, you may choose to make a Story a Category Index by checking the box to the right of the slug field. The URL of the Category plus the slug (if any) form the complete URL of the Story, which must be unique.
  • Cover Date: As on the New Story page, the cover Date defaults to the current date and time, but it can be modified to a future or previous date. The cover date is a required field and may appear on some Story Types directly in the URL or the page.
  • Current Version: Field set by the CMS. This is a non-editable field, reflects the version number of the Story.
  • Published Version: Version of the Story that was last published. This may be different from the Current Version if changes were made to the Story after the last publish. This field is controlled automatically by the CMS.
  • All Versions: You can revert to a previously saved version. Select the version number from the drop-down and click on Revert. If you prefer, you can also View a previously saved version.
  • Contributors: A contributor can be any person, ex. Writer or photographer who contributed to the Story. Click on Edit Contributors to associate, or un-associate your contributors. See Associate Contributors for additional Help.

Content

Below the Properties box, is the Content box. This is where you give shape to the Story, by editing its existing elements and adding new ones. Each different Story type has its own set of elements -- some are automatically included and some are optional.

Click the Edit button next to an element to go to its Element Profile page, which is where you can insert text and choose attributes. To include a new element, select one from the pulldown menu and click the Add Element button. You will automatically be sent to the new element's profile page, where you can enter its data.

  • Reorder: To change the order of the elements, use the pulldowns to adjust the position numbers, then click the Reorder button.
  • Delete: To delete an item, check the box next to it and the click the Delete button. You can delete more than one item at a time.

Sites/Categories

Categories determine where on the site an item is published. An item can live in one Category or in several, but only one is designated as the primary Category. On your first time through, a Story's only Category will be the one you chose on the New Story page. To add a Category, click on the Add Site/Category button and choose one from the list.

  • Primary: To choose a new Category as the primary one, click the circle next to it, then click on Save or Save and Stay at the bottom of the page.
  • Replace This Category: To replace a Category (i.e. move the Story to a new location), click the circle next to it and then click on Replace This Category at the bottom of the page.
  • Delete: To delete a Category, check the box next to it and then click the Delete button. You can delete more than one Category at a time.

Actions

At the bottom of the edit story page, there are buttons and different actions to choose from. Some of them are only available at the top level of a story; others are available only when editing a container element.

  • Publish: Click on Publish and your Story is published immediately. If you would like to schedule your Story to publish at a later date, click on Schedule at the top of the page. See Schedule Editor for more information.
  • Check In To [Desk] After completing your Story, you can Check it in for another editor. Select the desk you want to check the Story into and click Check In.
  • Delete: Click on Delete to delete your Story from the CMS.
  • Done With [Element]: Click on Done With [Element] to return to the previous level of the Story.
  • Delete [Element]: Click on Delete [Element] to remove the element from the Story.
  • Cancel: Click on Cancel to discard any unsaved changes and return the Story to its previous location.
  • Save & Stay: Click on Save & Stay to save your Story to disk and remain on the Edit Story screen.
  • Save: Click on Save to save your Story to disk and return to your Workspace.

In addition to the Action buttons featured at the bottom of the page, the following two actions are available at the top:

  • View Log: Please see Log for more information.
  • View Schedule: Please see Schedule for more information.
